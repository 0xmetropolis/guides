Does anything special happen at the 45th minute? (all weekend hours)

Hypothesis test using the last full collection window (Sat 2026-04-25 → Sun 2026-04-26 ET). The actual crash weekend (04-18/19) is not available — both HL parquet files were corrupted by the OOM and we have no replacement. This is a proxy.
Background. The HL recorder crashed mid-Saturday at 09:44 ET and again mid-Sunday at 10:45 ET. The proximate cause was an OOM kill — every flush re-loaded the entire daily Parquet file into memory, hitting the 256 MB container cap. Fixed in commit 08a8a7d. The question this report tries to answer: was the :44/:45 timing meaningful, or just the minute where the cumulative buffer happened to cross threshold?
Caveats up front.
  • n = 1 weekend. Patterns visible here might not generalise.
  • The crash itself was driven by cumulative file size, not per-minute activity. A market event at :45 only matters if it spikes activity enough to push a borderline-OOM container over the edge — and the postmortem shows the staircase was already 95.9% of cap before any final spike.
  • The 5-minute flush schedule lands on :04, :09, …, :44, :49, :54, :59. The 9th flush of the day is :44. Coincidence is plausible.

Section 3 · Null-hypothesis test — the flush staircase

Reconstructs the cumulative HL row count sampled at every :04, :09, …, :59 flush boundary across the weekend. The dashed line is the OOM threshold from the postmortem (136,164 rows triggered the kill on 04-18). If the OOM is purely a function of cumulative size, the crash will land on whichever :04+5k minute the line crosses the threshold — :44 wasn't selected by anything in the market, it was selected by the flush schedule.

How to read the result

Reading guide.
  • If H1–H5 charts are flat across all minutes (no visible spike at :44–:46) and the staircase chart shows the threshold getting crossed at the next :04+5k flush after the cumulative count exceeds it: the :45 hypothesis is unsupported. The original crashes are explained entirely by the cumulative-size bug + the 5-min flush cadence.
  • If H1–H2 spike at :44–:46 on Hyperliquid only (and not on Extended/Binance): a real HL-specific event at :45 is plausible — likely tied to Hyperliquid's hourly funding cycle (funding pays at :00, repositioning may bunch pre-funding at :45–:59).
  • If H5 (mark-trade gap) spikes at :45 on HL only: oracle update cadence may pause around :45, leaving trade price free to drift.
  • If patterns appear on all three venues: market-wide schedule (e.g., MM bots running cron, news-feed cycles, exchange-side snapshot intervals).
What this report cannot tell us. n = 1 weekend. Even a clean spike at :45 is suggestive, not confirmatory. The right next step if a pattern appears is to re-run the same aggregation across the next 4–5 weekends as data accumulates.
